Latency Optimization in Multi-Camera FRS Deployment (Retail Sector)

Worked on optimizing a face recognition and footfall analysis system across 30 RTSP-enabled entry/exit cameras deployed in a mall. Initially faced latency issues, with detections taking 20–30 seconds to appear.

My role involved structuring and assigning camera streams to index.py files (4 cameras per file), labeling them by type (entry/exit/zone), and conducting performance testing. I monitored GPU usage (nvidia-smi: perf state, volatile utilization), CPU load (htop: load avg, memory, threads, swap), and system response time for detections.

Through incremental testing — adding one camera at a time — I observed that performance remained stable up to 10 streams (~5–7s latency). Adding the 11th stream caused sharp increases in GPU usage, CPU load, and latency. After testing multiple configurations, the optimal setup was achieved by splitting the load into 2 index files with 15 cameras each, reducing detection latency across all 30 streams to ~10 seconds.
